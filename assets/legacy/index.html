OG Juice 2017-present

Client: OG Juice Ltd (working as an employee at Robot Mascot Ltd.)
OG Juice required a bespoke e-commerce website to process orders of raw organic juice. Working as part of a small team, I worked mainly on the backend of this site, both the code required to handle orders and the bespoke CMS requested by the client. Built using a Digital Ocean droplet, which is managed and maintained by myself, the website uses MongoDB, Stripe and JQuery (amongst other things) to provide a great customer experience and ease of use for the client.

Attom 2015-2017

Client: South East Cornwall Multi-Academy Regional Trust (SMART)
Attom is an attendance tracking system designed to register and monitor the signing in and out of sixth form students. The app was fully implemented at the saltash.net community school sixth form for year 12 and 13, before being replaced by a fingerprint-based system in 2017. It used NFC cards which were given to users and then swiped against an Android tablet which used a native Android app to process the swipe and output a custom audio and visual message to the user.

Users of the app commented on its ease of use and its speed compared to the old paper system, with almost 98% of students rating it good or outstanding as a system.

Attom is no longer available

Zappr 2013-2017

Client: South East Cornwall Multi-Academy Regional Trust (SMART)
Zappr is a peer-to-peer and traditional praise system for the SECRET skills. The system was used by year 7 students as part of their integrated learning curriculum and was also displayed to both Hillcrest Christian College (Queensland, AUS) and SIVECO Romania, both giving positive reviews of the software. Currently, a native Android app is in the late stages of development, along with a web platform, all designed (including graphics), developed and tested by myself. An Ubuntu server was set up by myself and run through appropriate CDNs to ensure security and speed.

The client has commented on several occasions about the quick turn around time of developments and professionalism throughout the planning, design, testing and evaluation phases of development.

PokéCounters Summer 2014

Completed for the YRS Festival of Code 2014 held in Plymouth, PokéCounters got through to the semi-finals of the competition under the 'Should Exist' category. After the YRSFOC, PokéCounters has been continually worked on by two out of the three other original team members and myself, and we believe that the application has never been faster or more accurate. The website uses an algorithm to compare Pokémon to see which Pokémon would best counter an enemy Pokémon, and a new feature which allows users to battle two Pokémon is now in Beta.
